## SECTION 5: POLICIES & EXPECTATIONS (3:30)

**[VISUAL: Canva policy cards displayed one at a time, with supporting photos of staff in proper uniform, clean workspace, etc.]**

**[TEXT ON SCREEN: Section card — "Policies & Expectations"]**

**[AI NARRATION]**

> Now let's talk about what's expected of you every time you walk through that door. These aren't suggestions — they're requirements. Following them shows respect for your team, our customers, and this business.

### Attendance & Punctuality

**[TEXT ON SCREEN: Policy card — "Attendance & Punctuality"]**

> **Attendance and punctuality.** When you're scheduled, you show up. On time means early. If you're walking in at your start time, you're already behind. If you need to call out, you contact your manager as far in advance as possible — not 10 minutes before your shift. Repeated no-shows or late arrivals will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. This is a small team. When one person doesn't show up, everyone else feels it.

### Dress Code & Hygiene

**[TEXT ON SCREEN: Policy card — "Dress Code & Hygiene"]**

> **Dress code and hygiene.** You will be told what to wear. Wear it. Your uniform should be clean, pressed, and complete every shift. No exceptions. Personal hygiene is non-negotiable in food service. Clean hands, clean nails, hair pulled back or covered. If you're working the kitchen, you follow every food safety hygiene standard we train you on. If you're working the front, you are the first thing customers see — look the part.

### Phone Policy

**[TEXT ON SCREEN: Policy card — "Phone Policy"]**

> **Phone policy.** Your phone goes away when you clock in. Not in your back pocket, not face-down on the counter — put away. If you need to make an emergency call, step away and let your manager know. Customers should never see you on your phone. Your attention belongs to the people in this restaurant.

### Attitude & Conduct

**[TEXT ON SCREEN: Policy card — "Attitude & Conduct"]**

> **Attitude and conduct.** You represent J Rodgers every second you're on this property. That means no gossiping in front of customers, no arguing with coworkers where anyone can hear, and absolutely no disrespectful behavior toward customers, managers, or teammates. If you have a problem, bring it to your manager privately. We handle things professionally here.

> Drama stays outside. When you walk through that door, you're at work, and our customers deserve your full, positive energy.
